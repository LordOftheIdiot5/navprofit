# NavProfit — Business Model

---

## The problem we solve

Small maritime operators (1-10 vessels) run their financial operations on spreadsheets, WhatsApp and gut feel. They don't know their voyage P&L until the voyage is over. They overpay for bunkers because they don't have price visibility. They lose track of invoices. Enterprise software like Softmar or Veson costs thousands per month and requires implementation teams.

**NavProfit gives them live financial intelligence at a price they can afford.**

---

## Target customer

**Primary:** Norwegian coastal and short-sea operators
- Fishing vessel owners with 2-5 boats
- Coastal cargo operators
- Offshore supply vessel companies (small, 1-5 vessels)
- Ferry/passenger operators on shorter routes

**Secondary (later):**
- Nordic maritime operators (Denmark, Sweden, Finland)
- North Sea operators (UK, Netherlands)
- Any small operator globally running 1-20 vessels

## Go-to-market

### Step 1 — Direct outreach (0-10 operators)
- Identify 20 small Norwegian operators via Kystverket vessel registry
- Personal outreach — email or phone
- Offer 3 months free in exchange for feedback
- Goal: 5 paying operators by end of month 3

### Step 2 — Maritime community (10-50 operators)
- Norwegian maritime associations (Norges Rederiforbund, Fiskebåt)
- Fishing industry networks in Trøndelag and Nordland
- Word of mouth within operator community
- Content marketing: "How much is fuel price variance costing you?"

### Step 3 — Partners (50-200 operators)
- Partnerships with Norwegian maritime brokers
- Integration with Norwegian accounting software (Tripletex, Visma)
- Ship chandlers and fuel suppliers as referral partners

## Second revenue stream (later)

Once we have data from hundreds of operators:
- **Anonymized market intelligence** — what are operators actually paying for bunkers in Norwegian ports? Banks and insurers will pay for this.
- **Freight rate benchmarking** — what are similar voyages earning? Valuable for brokers.
- **Credit scoring for maritime lending** — banks want voyage profitability data for loan decisions.

This data product could eventually be worth more than the SaaS subscription revenue.

---

## Risks

**Adoption risk** — maritime is conservative. Mitigation: free trial, personal onboarding, extremely simple UI.

**Data risk** — operators may not trust a new platform with financial data. Mitigation: Norwegian company, GDPR compliant, clear data policy, local hosting option.

**Competition risk** — large players could build downmarket. Mitigation: move fast, build community loyalty, integrate deeply with Norwegian-specific workflows (Tripletex, Altinn reporting).

**Regulatory** — we are not a payment processor, just analytics. Low regulatory burden.
